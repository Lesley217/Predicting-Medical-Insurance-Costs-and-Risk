Predicting Medical Insurance Costs and Risk: A Comparative Modeling Study

This is my course project for the course DSAI5102 Principles of Data Science of The Hong Kong Polytechnic University. 

From previous studies, there was a global life expectancy rise from 65.5 years in 1990 to 73.3 years in 2019 (Bloom et al., 2015) and it was predicted that there will be a rise in medical expenditures. Medical Insurance was claiming to be protecting us from sudden financial risks but many companies adopt conservative pricing and deny claims. Hence, this project aimed at building a scientific and accurate medical insurance payout model for better customer decision and also help companies form better business models at the same time.

Past studies has used machine learning models for analysis. For instance, there was an accuracy score of 91.25% with LightGBM model for predicting high risk individuals (Maisog et al. 2019). M5 decision trees, random forest and generalized boosting models had high accuracy in predicting annual health costs as well(Duncan et al. 2016). Yet, there was a lack of research focusing both predicting annual medical costs and classifying prominent causes in a single study
